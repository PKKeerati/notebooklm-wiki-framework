# MLIPs for Mg-H Phase Transitions: Gaps and Strategic Directions

## Key Insights

> [!important] **No MLIP benchmarks exist for Mg-H phase transitions**
> Machine Learning Interatomic Potentials (MLIPs) such as [[Gaussian Approximation Potential (GAP)]], [[Neural Network Potential (NNP)]], [[M3GNet]], and [[NequIP]] have been applied to Mg-H systems primarily for hydrogen diffusion studies, but their accuracy for the [[Mg-hcp ↔ MgH₂-rutile phase transition]] or the reaction [[Mg + H₂ → MgH₂]] remains untested. While DFT-derived energy-volume curves and elastic constants are commonly used for validation, these metrics do not capture the full complexity of phase transitions, including reaction energetics, phonon spectra, and hysteresis. The lack of benchmarks limits the selection of optimal MLIPs for hydrogen storage simulations, as performance in diffusion studies does not necessarily translate to accuracy in phase stability or transition kinetics. Establishing such benchmarks is critical for advancing MLIP-driven simulations of Mg-H systems.

> [!important] **μNPT/GCMC frameworks are absent for Mg-H hydrogen storage simulations**
> Grand Canonical Monte Carlo ([[μNPT ensemble]]) and hybrid μNPT-Molecular Dynamics ([[MD]]) workflows have not been implemented for Mg-H systems, despite their necessity for modeling hydrogen absorption and desorption under variable chemical potential ($\mu_{H_2}$). Current simulations rely on [[NPT-MD]], which fixes the number of hydrogen atoms and cannot capture equilibrium conditions under realistic storage scenarios. The absence of μNPT frameworks for Mg-H systems precludes accurate simulations of hydrogen uptake and release dynamics, as these processes are inherently governed by chemical potential. Defining chemical potential ranges, system sizes, and calibration protocols for Mg-H is essential for developing robust μNPT workflows.

> [!important] **PCA has not been applied to analyze Mg-H phase transitions**
> [[Principal Component Analysis (PCA)]] and other [[dimensionality reduction]] techniques have not been used to analyze the [[Mg-hcp ↔ MgH₂-rutile phase transition]] in MD trajectories. PCA could identify order parameters or collective variables (e.g., lattice strain, hydrogen coordination) that distinguish the hcp and rutile phases, providing mechanistic insights into the transition. However, no studies have applied PCA to Mg-H MD data, leaving the phase transition mechanisms poorly characterized. Alternative techniques such as [[Kernel PCA]] or [[autoencoders]] may be required to capture non-linear effects, but their application to Mg-H systems remains unexplored.

> [!important] **No Mg-H-specific MLIP fine-tuning protocols exist**
> While general [[fine-tuning]] methods (e.g., [[transfer learning]], [[active learning]], hyperparameter optimization) are available for MLIPs, protocols tailored to Mg-H systems have not been documented. Mg-H systems present unique challenges due to the strong coupling between lattice strain and hydrogen diffusion, which may require specialized fine-tuning approaches. The absence of Mg-H-specific protocols risks overfitting to DFT data, reducing the generalizability of MLIPs to experimental conditions (e.g., defects, surfaces, or grain boundaries). Developing such protocols is critical for improving MLIP accuracy in phase transition and reaction energetics simulations.

> [!important] **Validation metrics for Mg-H MLIPs are undefined or untested**
> Critical performance metrics for MLIPs in Mg-H systems, such as reaction energy barriers for [[Mg + H₂ → MgH₂]], phase transition hysteresis, and [[phonon spectra]], have not been validated against [[DFT]] or experimental data. While root-mean-square error (RMSE) for energy and forces is commonly reported, these metrics do not assess the accuracy of MLIPs in reactive or phase-transition scenarios. Phonon spectra, which govern thermal stability, are rarely validated, and experimental data (e.g., pressure-composition isotherms) are sparse. Defining and validating these metrics is essential for ensuring MLIPs meet the performance criteria required for hydrogen storage applications.

> [!warning] **Anharmonic effects in Mg-H phase transitions are unexplored**
> The role of [[anharmonicity]], including phonon-phonon coupling, thermal expansion, and other non-harmonic effects, in the kinetics of the [[Mg-hcp ↔ MgH₂-rutile phase transition]] has not been studied. DFT and MLIPs typically assume [[harmonic approximation]]s, which may fail to capture critical dynamics at high temperatures or near phase transitions. For example, thermal expansion coefficients and phonon lifetimes in MgH₂ are unknown, limiting the accuracy of kinetic models. Advanced methods such as [[quasiharmonic approximation]]s or [[self-consistent phonon]] techniques could address this gap, but their application to Mg-H systems remains speculative. Exploring anharmonic effects is necessary for improving the fidelity of MLIP-driven simulations.

> [!warning] **GAP and NNP are the most studied MLIPs for Mg-H diffusion, but not phase transitions**
> [[Gaussian Approximation Potential (GAP)]] and [[Neural Network Potential (NNP)]] models trained on DFT data have demonstrated reasonable accuracy for hydrogen diffusion barriers in Mg matrices. However, their performance for the [[Mg-hcp ↔ MgH₂-rutile phase transition]] or reaction energetics remains untested. These models may lack transferability due to limited training data for high-energy configurations, such as those encountered during phase transitions. While their success in diffusion studies is promising, benchmarking their accuracy for phase stability and reaction energetics is necessary before they can be reliably applied to hydrogen storage simulations.

> [!warning] **M3GNet and NequIP lack validation for Mg-H systems**
> [[M3GNet]] and [[NequIP]], two state-of-the-art MLIP architectures, have not been validated for Mg-H systems, despite their success in modeling phase transitions in other materials (e.g., Li-Si, MoS₂). M3GNet, a graph-based MLIP, and NequIP, an equivariant neural network, have shown promise for capturing complex interatomic interactions, but their accuracy for Mg-H reaction energetics, phonon spectra, and phase stability is speculative. Their transferability to Mg-H systems remains untested, and benchmarking their performance is a critical step for advancing MLIP-driven simulations in this domain.

> [!important] **DFT benchmarks may not align with experimental reaction barriers**
> DFT-derived reaction energy barriers for the reaction [[Mg + H₂ → MgH₂]] may not match experimental values due to limitations in [[exchange-correlation functional]]s. For example, functionals such as [[PBE]] and [[RPBE]] often underestimate reaction barriers or overestimate stability due to self-interaction errors. Discrepancies of 0.1–0.3 eV between DFT and experimental barriers are common, which can significantly impact the accuracy of MLIPs trained on DFT data. Hybrid functionals (e.g., [[HSE06]]) or [[GW corrections]] may improve accuracy but are computationally expensive. Calibrating DFT benchmarks against experimental data is essential for ensuring the reliability of MLIPs in Mg-H systems.

> [!important] **μNPT simulations require calibration of hydrogen chemical potential**
> μNPT simulations for Mg-H systems require precise calibration of the hydrogen chemical potential ($\mu_{H_2}$) to experimental pressure-composition isotherms. The chemical potential governs hydrogen absorption and desorption equilibrium, and its value must be derived from experimental data or DFT calculations. For Mg-H systems, $\mu_{H_2}$ typically ranges from -0.5 to -0.2 eV at temperatures of 300–600 K, but exact values depend on temperature and pressure. Incorrect calibration of $\mu_{H_2}$ can lead to unrealistic uptake kinetics or equilibrium conditions, undermining the validity of μNPT simulations. Establishing robust calibration protocols is critical for developing accurate μNPT workflows.

> [!warning] **PCA may fail to capture non-linear phase transition variables**
> Linear dimensionality reduction techniques such as [[Principal Component Analysis (PCA)]] may not identify non-linear collective variables critical to the [[Mg-hcp ↔ MgH₂-rutile phase transition]]. PCA assumes linear correlations between features (e.g., atomic positions, lattice parameters), but phase transitions often involve non-linear couplings, such as hydrogen-hydrogen interactions or lattice strain. Techniques like [[Kernel PCA]] or [[autoencoders]] may better capture these non-linear effects, but they require larger datasets and computational resources. The risk of PCA failing to identify key order parameters highlights the need for alternative dimensionality reduction methods in Mg-H phase transition analysis.
